Project Plan: Senior AI Engineer Candidate Ranker

Build a reproducible CPU-only ranker that selects the top 100 candidates from 100,000 profiles for Redrob AI's founding Senior AI Engineer role. The core bet is simple: rank candidates by evidence of shipped retrieval, ranking, search, and applied ML systems in product environments, then adjust for availability, location, culture fit, and profile consistency.

Recommended Approach

Build a deterministic hybrid ranker first. Use structured signals, lexical evidence, and carefully weighted heuristics. Add optional local embeddings only if they are fully cached and reproducible. Do not rely on live LLM calls, broad keyword counts, or one-shot prompt scoring.

1. Candidate Fit

Score role evidence: retrieval/search/ranking systems, vector databases, evaluation frameworks, Python, production deployment, product-company shipping, and hands-on coding.

2. Hiring Reality

Modify by Redrob behavioral signals: recent activity, recruiter response, response speed, open-to-work flag, notice period, verification, location, and relocation.

3. Risk Control

Penalize keyword stuffing, service-only career histories, pure research, irrelevant AI domains, impossible profiles, stale candidates, and reasoning hallucinations.

Target Candidate Profile

The JD is for a Senior AI Engineer on a founding team. The right ranking should read between the lines and avoid "AI keyword density" as the main signal.

Strong Positive Evidence

  • 5 to 9 years total experience, with flexibility for exceptional 4 or 10+ year profiles.
  • Production search, recommendation, retrieval, ranking, or matching systems shipped to real users.
  • Embeddings-based retrieval, hybrid search, vector database, or search infrastructure operations.
  • Evaluation literacy: NDCG, MRR, MAP, A/B testing, offline benchmarks, regression tracking.
  • Strong Python and hands-on production coding in the last 18 months.
  • Product-company experience, especially SaaS, AI/ML, marketplace, fintech, edtech, ecommerce, or data products.
  • Scrappy product-engineering orientation: shipped working systems, iterated with users, owned production quality.
  • India-based, preferably Pune, Noida, Delhi NCR, Hyderabad, Mumbai, or Bangalore, or willing to relocate.
  • Recent platform engagement and clear ability to respond to recruiters.

Negative Evidence

  • Career only at consulting or IT services firms: TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Accenture, Cognizant, Capgemini, HCL, Mindtree.
  • Pure research with no production deployment.
  • Only recent LangChain/OpenAI wrapper projects without pre-LLM ML/search depth.
  • Architecture-only leadership with no recent coding.
  • Primary experience in computer vision, speech, robotics, marketing, HR, accounting, support, or operations without serious NLP/IR evidence.
  • Job hopping focused on title escalation, especially every 12 to 18 months.
  • Inactive candidates: stale login, low response rate, long response time, not open to work, high notice period.
  • Suspicious or impossible profile details that may indicate honeypots.
Interpretation rule: A plain-language profile that says "built a recommendation service and ranking evaluation for a marketplace" is stronger than a profile listing RAG, Pinecone, and LoRA as skills with no career evidence.

Ranking Strategy

Scoring Philosophy

Use a transparent score that can be defended in an interview and explained row by row. Separate "can do the job" from "can be hired now."

Optimize top-of-list precision because NDCG@10 has 50% weight and P@10 is the final tiebreak ladder after P@5.

final_score = 100 * (
  0.42 * role_depth
  + 0.18 * production_evidence
  + 0.12 * search_retrieval_match
  + 0.08 * evaluation_maturity
  + 0.08 * seniority_fit
  + 0.06 * product_company_fit
  + 0.06 * location_logistics_fit
) * behavioral_modifier - risk_penalty

Recommended Candidate Funnel

  1. Parse and normalize. Build normalized text fields from current role, summary, headline, career descriptions, skill names, education, company, and industry.
  2. Hard negative screen. Apply strong penalties for irrelevant current roles, service-only career, pure non-NLP AI domains, stale availability, and impossible profile signals.
  3. Evidence scoring. Score direct career evidence above skill tags. Current and recent roles get more weight than old roles.
  4. Behavioral modifier. Apply a multiplier for open-to-work, recency, response rate, response time, notice period, verification, and recruiter saves.
  5. Manual audit set. Export top 200 with feature breakdowns, inspect top 50 and borderline 80 to 130, adjust weights only with documented rationale.
  6. Freeze and generate. Produce final top 100, reasoning strings, metadata, and validation artifacts.

Behavioral Modifier

Use Redrob signals as a multiplier, not as the main relevance score. The JD says a perfect-on-paper inactive candidate is not actually available.

Trap & Honeypot Defense

The bundle warns about keyword stuffers, plain-language Tier 5s, behavioral twins, and roughly 80 honeypots with subtly impossible profiles. Submissions with honeypot rate above 10% in the top 100 are disqualified at Stage 3.

Trap Types To Detect

  • Keyword stuffer: many AI skills but non-technical career history or unrelated title.
  • Plain-language strong fit: fewer buzzwords but built search, ranking, or recommender systems.
  • Behavioral twin: similar technical profile but one candidate is available and responsive while the other is stale.
  • Impossible profile: dates, skill durations, seniority, company age, or assessment patterns do not make sense.

Automated Checks

  • Skill density without career evidence: penalize when AI skill count is high but career descriptions lack production/search/retrieval terms.
  • Experience consistency: compare years of experience to career durations and title seniority.
  • Skill duration sanity: expert skill with zero months, many expert skills with tiny durations, or assessment scores that contradict claimed expertise.
  • Date sanity: current role date, signup date, last active date, career end dates, and education years.
  • Service-only career screen: all companies in explicit disfavored consulting list.
  • Availability floor: stale + low response + long notice should rarely enter top 100.
Do not special-case exact honeypot IDs. Build general consistency penalties and manually inspect the final top 100 for suspicious rows.

Risk and trap penalties

Add consistency checks, service-only penalty, non-NLP AI penalty, keyword-stuffer penalty, stale behavior penalty, and high-notice penalty.

Reasoning generator

Generate 1 to 2 sentence reasons from the strongest positive facts and the main concern. Restrict wording to facts present in the candidate record.

Manual audit workflow

Export top 200 with all component scores. Inspect top 50 and ranks 80 to 130. Adjust weights in feature_weights.json with a changelog.

Final packaging

Run validator, runtime benchmark, sample sandbox test, README reproduction, metadata completion, and final CSV generation.

Runtime Design

  • Stream candidates.jsonl line by line and keep only a fixed-size heap or list of candidate scores. 100,000 records is small enough to keep compact score dicts in memory.
  • Precompile regex patterns and normalize text once per candidate.
  • Use only standard library plus optional lightweight dependencies such as numpy, pandas, or scikit-learn if they are pinned and justified.
  • Make output deterministic: fixed config, stable sorting, no randomness unless seeded and documented.
  • Write audit artifacts separately from the final CSV so the submitted file stays exactly four columns.

Reasoning Generation Plan

The reasoning column is optional but strongly recommended and used in Stage 4. It should be generated from structured facts, not by a language model hallucinating profile details.

Reasoning Template Logic

positive_facts = [
  strongest_role_fact,
  strongest_retrieval_or_ranking_fact,
  strongest_production_or_evaluation_fact,
  strongest_behavior_or_location_fact
]
concern = highest_penalty_reason if meaningful
reasoning = one sentence of fit + one short concern or availability note

Examples Of Acceptable Tone

  • Rank 1 to 10: confident, specific, and direct about production search/ranking evidence.
  • Rank 11 to 50: strong but acknowledge a minor concern such as location, notice period, or missing GitHub.
  • Rank 51 to 100: realistic and bounded, such as "adjacent fit" or "strong data engineering base but less explicit ranking ownership."
Never mention a skill, employer, location, assessment, or years of experience unless it is present in the candidate record or computed directly from it.

Suggested 200-Word Methodology Summary

Use this as a starting point, then edit it to match the final implementation.

Deterministic hybrid ranker for the Senior AI Engineer JD. The model scores direct career evidence of production retrieval, search, ranking, recommendation, embeddings, vector infrastructure, Python, and evaluation systems, with career-history evidence weighted above skill tags to avoid AI-keyword stuffing. A separate logistics and behavior modifier incorporates Redrob signals such as recent activity, open-to-work status, recruiter response rate, response time, notice period, verification, and location or relocation fit. Risk penalties demote service-only careers, pure research profiles, irrelevant AI domains, stale candidates, inconsistent skill-duration claims, and other honeypot-like anomalies. Final rankings are generated by stable sorting on score and candidate ID, then reasonings are built from the highest-scoring factual evidence and the main concern for each candidate. The ranking step streams the 100K JSONL file, runs fully on CPU without network access, and produces a validated top-100 CSV with a single command.

Pivotal Decision Points

These are the places where the project direction can change. The recommended option is conservative and competition-aligned.

Decision Option A Option B Recommendation
Ranking architecture Pure deterministic rules: fastest, transparent, easy to defend. Local embeddings plus rules: better semantic recall, more artifact/dependency complexity. Start with rules, add local embeddings only after baseline validation.
Manual audit scope Audit only top 100. Audit top 200 and ranks 80 to 130 for cutoff mistakes. Audit top 200. Cutoff errors can hurt NDCG@50 and MAP.
Behavioral weighting Small modifier so strong technical profiles remain high. Large modifier so only responsive candidates rank high. Use a capped modifier. The JD requires technical fit first but explicitly says inactive candidates should be down-weighted.
Sandbox platform HuggingFace Spaces or Streamlit Cloud with sample upload. Docker image and run command. Use Streamlit or HuggingFace if the UI is simple; Docker if dependency reproducibility matters more.
Reasoning style Template from structured facts. LLM-written explanations. Template from facts. It is safer, deterministic, and avoids hallucinations.
